Key Insights

1.Customer Revenue Concentration
  A significant portion of total revenue is generated by a small group of high-value customers, indicating a strong dependency on key customers.
  
2.City-wise Revenue Distribution
  Revenue is unevenly distributed across cities. Birj contributes the highest share, while Delhi shows comparatively lower contribution, highlighting regional      performance differences.
  
3.Customer Behavior Patterns
  Customer engagement varies significantly. Some customers place multiple high-value orders, whereas others remain inactive, indicating an opportunity for          targeted re-engagement strategies.
  
4.Sales Trends
  Sales exhibit noticeable day-to-day fluctuations, suggesting inconsistent demand patterns and the possible influence of external factors.
  
5.Operational Efficiency
  The delivery success rate is approximately 90%, reflecting generally efficient operations. However, there is still scope to improve handling of failed or         cancelled orders.
  
Final Conclusions

Revenue is highly concentrated among a few key customers.

Certain cities outperform others significantly.

There is untapped potential in reactivating inactive customers.

Sales patterns are inconsistent across different days.

Operational efficiency is strong but can be further improved.

Expanding efforts in low-performing regions can drive overall growth.
